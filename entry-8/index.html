Silver Winds

Paragraph 1

Forever by each other's side with wings beating in succinct harmony,

I feel overshadowed, by the angle of the sun, and nevertheless, by you.

Invariably united from nascency, an imperfect dyad.

My wings, our pride, will never match yours.

Your consoling never shows conviction, your sympathy never evinces candor.




Paragraph 2

But on the day of the storm, you were torn away from me.

The silver wind blows, contorting your body within the whirlwind of debris.

In solace and protection, you took my weaker side.

Despite struggle and struggle and struggle, you invigorated a contingency.

A martyr, for me.




Paragraph 3

Turning from the kaleidoscope, radiant within your shadow.

I found you, still clutching to your spirit.

I'm sorry.

I'm sorry I doubted you, I’m sorry I hated myself.

Triumphant, glittering wings,

Return to the sky.

The silver of your forwings glow,

Eternally beautiful, beside mine.
