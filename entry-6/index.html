Winter on the Shorefront

Paragraph 1

The footprints I set in the sand on the coast,

Are covered up gently by the delicate snowfall.

My childhood house,

Sitting on its perch just shy of the cliffside,

Revels in the glory of the first light of dawn.

A trial,

Set by my boots,

Leads to the oak front door.

The secret route, shared only between me and the home,

Slowly becomes lost within surrounding tall grass.

As the wheels lurch forward, the wheel of fate turns.

The window, steamed by my breath, covers my view of the house.

Behind the fog, destiny smiles on the house,

Sunshine enveloping its walls, pouring into the emptied rooms.

My secrets, my home, my childhood.

Remain intact,

In the walls of my mind.
