Losing

Paragraph 1

Where the light of the flame nor the depths can reach you,

You’re spinning under curtains of water, skinning and flying,

All together, at the same time.

That's how I see you.

Tumbling and turning further and further away from me.

With no direction but away.



Paragraph 2

Everyday that we spend together,

I see you slipping.

How does it feel?

Do you even notice?

I notice the disconnect,

A disunity.

Estrangement from who you used to be,

Who we used to be.



Paragraph 3

The light of the flame, engulfed by the depths,

Extinguish under the pressure.

And no matter how much I reach out,

With fingers outstretched,

I’m unable to reclaim even a piece of you.
