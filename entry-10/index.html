Falling for You

Paragraph 1

As I wait for the fall,

To hand you my letter,

My eloquence fails me again and again.

Cyclical whirring, my words prenatal

Through tar and feather, churning through mud

Humility, and confidence, the scales tip, and rest.



Paragraph 2

Howling, billowing, autumnal leaves dance before my eyes,

Clear skies, as wind gives rise to a path between turbulent clouds,

A mailbox shines from the sun, daring me to approach.

Clutched in my hands, clasped to my chest,

I release a breath.



Paragraph 3

As you leave to another life,

I hope my letter stays with you,

I should have said something sooner,

But I couldn't muster the courage to say it to you,

I just wanted you to know, I hope my feelings ring true.

You don’t have to write me back,

But I’ll still count the days,

The clouds roll over, blocking the sun's rays.
