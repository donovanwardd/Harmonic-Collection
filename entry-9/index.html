Foliage

Paragraph 1

Heir to the throne.

When I’m near you the flowers start to wither.

Yet I desire it everso.

Wilting willows, out of the corners of our eyes, draw our gazes away from the interlocked warfare.

Snared by roots and vines, I’m able to claw my way out of the brush, away from your touch.

The maze of shrubbery imprisoning me for so long,

Bathing in the light,

Please, please save me.

Please let me savor the warmth a little longer.

Please oh please, save him and me.

Over the moss covered wall,

And past the brambles,

A clearing in the trees reveals the town at the trough of the hill.

From one arrangement into standing among others,

Flowers spot the ground, beaming with youthful innocence.
